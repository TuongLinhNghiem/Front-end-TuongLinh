What is CS50x?

CS50 is Harvard University's introductory computer science course, and it's very well-known in the field of computer science education. It's a popular course both at Harvard and online, with millions of enrollments on edX. The course is known for its challenging problem sets, focus on fundamental concepts, and the engaging teaching style of its instructor, David J. Malan.

My CS50x's journey

This course has a duration of 11 weeks (3 months), however, it only took more than 1 month for me to complete it. Every lecture ranges from 2 hours to 3 hours covers a wide range of topics, from foundational concepts like algorithms and data structures to practical skills like web development and database management (shout out to Prof Malan's energetic and clear lectures which made those lectures became more engaging to me). The weekly problem sets are designed to be challenging and encourage students to apply their knowledge and develop their programming skills.

This is Profesor David Malan

He is an American computer scientist and professor. Mr. Malan is a Gordon McKay Professor of Computer Science at Harvard University and is best known for teaching the course CS50.
